Introduction

My projects focus is manga or Japanese comic books/graphic novels.

Ever year, there are lots of manga that come out of Japan either brand-new or continuing from the years prior. While some manga succeed or stay succeeding, a lot of other manga are cancelled or left on hiatus early in its run. Not all factors that lend to this is in the hands of the people working on it.

My questions:
- What variables make for a successful manga series?
- Can we predict which manga series will be successful?

Data

I got both of my datasets from Kaggle

  • MyAnimeList (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/andreuvallhernndez/myanimelist) by Andreu Vall Hernndez. Has 64,833 rows due to having the whole websites database of manga.
  • Best-Selling Manga (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/drahulsingh/best-selling-manga) by D Rahulsingh. A dataset of 187 manga.

Conclusion

The three variables I decided to look: if theres an anime adaptation, its genres, and its themes where all shown to have an affect on the success of a new manga to varies degree.

Anime adaptations: 17:82 for best selling mangas without/with an adaptation. (~82.82%) Genres & Themes: there are clear subject topics that a lot of the best selling mangas fall under
